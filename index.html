About Me

Hello, there! My name is Naigam. I currently work at American Express, AI Labs as a Research Engineer. My work focuses on two main areas: research and development in the largely untapped topic of explanability in the unstructured world, and contributing to development of American Express’ internal tools like AXGBoost, an extension of open-source XGBoost, and PyPMML evaluator. Prior to this, I’ve also worked as a student-researcher in APPCAIR Labs in collaboration with Reflexis Colab, under the supervision of Prof. Ashwin Srinivasan and Tirtharaj Dash, which apart from the obvious learnings, enlightened and introduced me to industrial standards of using Deep Learning tools.

I graduated from BITS Pilani, K.K. Birla Goa campus with a bachelor’s degree in Computer Science. Over a period of 4 years there, I’ve acquired a strong interest in the domains of machine and deep learning, and have completed a number of courses to broaden my understanding in this area. To begin, I worked as a research intern at CEERI, Pilani, under the supervision of Dr. Sanjay Singh, which exposed me to real-world applications of Deep Learning. Apart from that, working on a number of projects in the fields of Computer Vision, Deep Reinforcement Learning & Malware detection alongwith multiple TAships have helped me gain more experience in this field.

Because the use of machine learning models as end-to-end systems is rising at an exponential rate, the concept of explaining them and making the decision process as transparent as possible becomes crucial and it has caught my attention. As a result, currently I’m researching and creating ways that will allow us to obtain insight into the workings of allegedly black box models, making the entire decision-making process more robust and transparent. If accepted and practiced widely, this will make the use of machine learning in practically all disciplines, more dependable and trustworthy.

In my free time, I enjoy swinging by the cricket ground, strumming my guitar, learning new things, and occasionally killing someone in an e-sports game.

LIME

LIME is a local interpretability technique that uses a simpler surrogate model like linear regression or decision tree, to explain the original complex target model**. It is a **model agnostic** method, meaning it does not depend on the underlying nature of the target model, and that makes this technique very flexible to accommodate any kind of machine/deep learning architectures. The way LIME works is: The algorithm takes in the local instance which you want to get explanations for.
